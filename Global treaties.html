Global Environmental Treaties

Many countries have signed international agreements to protect the environment:

    The Paris Agreement (2015)

    Objective: To combat climate change by limiting global temperature rise to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels, with efforts to limit the increase to 1.5°C.

    Kyoto Protocol (1997)

    Objective: To reduce greenhouse gas emissions based on the premise that global warming exists and human-made CO₂ emissions have caused it.

    The Montreal Protocol (1987)

    Objective: To protect the ozone layer by phasing out the production and consumption of substances that deplete the ozone layer, especially chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs).

    Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD) (1992)

    Objective: To conserve biological diversity, promote sustainable use of its components, and ensure fair and equitable sharing of benefits arising from genetic resources.

    The Ramsar Convention on Wetlands (1971)

    Objective: To conserve and sustainably use wetlands, recognizing their ecological functions and economic, cultural, scientific, and recreational value.

    The United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) (1992)

    Objective: To stabilize greenhouse gas concentrations in the atmosphere to prevent dangerous interference with the climate system.

    The Basel Convention (1989)

    Objective: To control and regulate the transboundary movement of hazardous waste and its disposal.

    The Stockholm Convention on Persistent Organic Pollutants (POPs) (2001)

    Objective: To eliminate or reduce the release of persistent organic pollutants (POPs), which are chemicals that remain in the environment for long periods, accumulate in living organisms, and pose risks to human health and the environment.

    The Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES) (1973)

    Objective: To regulate international trade in endangered species to ensure that it does not threaten their survival.

    The Minamata Convention on Mercury (2013)

    Objective: To protect human health and the environment from anthropogenic emissions and releases of mercury and mercury compounds.

    The Cartagena Protocol on Biosafety (2000)

    Objective: To ensure the safe handling, transfer, and use of living modified organisms (LMOs) resulting from modern biotechnology, focusing on transboundary movements.

    Vienna Convention for the Protection of the Ozone Layer (1985)

    Objective: To protect human health and the environment against the adverse effects resulting from the depletion of the ozone layer.
