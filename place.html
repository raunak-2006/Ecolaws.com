Wildlife Sanctuaries(565)

Jim Corbett National Park

Located in Uttarakhand, it is India's oldest national park, famous for Bengal tigers and the first to come under Project Tiger initiative.

Kaziranga National Park

Located in Assam, this UNESCO World Heritage site is home to two-thirds of the world's one-horned rhinoceroses.

Ranthambore Wildlife Sanctuary

Situated in Rajasthan, Ranthambore is known for its significant tiger population and ancient ruins scattered around the forest.

Gir Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Gujarat, Gir is the only place in the world where Asiatic lions can be found in their natural habitat.

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in Kerala, Periyar is famous for its elephant population and its picturesque lake surrounded by thick forests.

Sundarbans Wildlife Sanctuary

Situated in West Bengal, the Sundarbans is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger and has the world's largest mangrove forests.

Biosphere Reserves(18)

Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve

Spanning Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Karnataka, it is known for its rich flora and fauna, including the Nilgiri Tahr and Indian elephant.

Sundarbans Biosphere Reserve

Located in West Bengal, it is famous for the Royal Bengal tiger and the world's largest mangrove forest.

Gulf of Mannar Biosphere Reserve

Situated in Tamil Nadu, this marine biosphere reserve is home to diverse coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds.

Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve

Located in Uttarakhand, it features the Nanda Devi and Valley of Flowers National Parks, both UNESCO World Heritage sites.

Pachmarhi Biosphere Reserve

Located in Madhya Pradesh, it is known for its biodiversity and the presence of many endemic species of plants and animals.

Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve

Situated in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, it is known for its tropical rainforests and unique wildlife such as the Nicobar pigeon.

Simlipal Biosphere Reserve

Located in Odisha, it is a critical habitat for tigers, elephants, and several other endangered species.

Achanakmar-Amarkantak Biosphere Reserve

Spanning Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh, it is a rich forest reserve with a wide range of plant and animal diversity.

Agasthyamala Biosphere Reserve

Spanning Tamil Nadu and Kerala, it is home to rare medicinal plants and the Agasthyamala hills, a hotspot for biodiversity.

Khangchendzonga Biosphere Reserve

Located in Sikkim, it includes Mt. Khangchendzonga, the third-highest peak in the world, and supports diverse ecosystems.
